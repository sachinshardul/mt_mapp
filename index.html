Training Video 02 : Introduction to the Sternum


THE STERNUM :

The anterior median part of the thoracic skeleton, is formed by the sternum. which is a flat bone. The sternum is about 17cm (7 inches) long. In Shape the sternum resembles the overall structure of a tie. The upper part of the tie also known as the knot corresponds to the upper part of the sternum which is called the manubrium. The middle part resembling the Flap of the tie is called the body of sternum, and the lowest tapering part forming the tip of the tie is the xiphoid process or xiphisternum. The sternum is longer in male whereas its shorter in female.
